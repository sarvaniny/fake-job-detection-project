Job Scam Knowledge Base

Registration Fee Scam

Overview: Fraudsters offer fake jobs but ask candidates to pay registration or training fees before starting work.

Warning Signs:

  • Recruiter asks for payment before hiring
  • Unrealistic salary promises
  • Recruiter using Gmail or Yahoo email

Crypto Investment Job Scam

Overview: Fake recruiters promise profits through cryptocurrency trading jobs.

Warning Signs:

  • Guaranteed investment returns
  • Requests for crypto deposits
  • Pressure to invest quickly

Work From Home Data Entry Scam

Overview: Scammers advertise easy online jobs with unrealistic earnings.

Warning Signs:

  • No experience required
  • Very high salary for simple work
  • Payment required to start work

Fake Interview Scam

Overview: Fraudsters conduct fake interviews and later request payment for equipment or training.

Warning Signs:

  • Interview only through chat
  • Offer provided immediately after interview
  • Requests payment for equipment

Task Scam

Overview: Victims are asked to complete simple online tasks and invest money to unlock higher payouts.

Warning Signs:

  • Telegram or WhatsApp recruiters
  • Investment required to continue tasks
  • Promises of daily earnings

Fake Recruiter Impersonation Scam

Overview: Scammers pretend to represent well-known companies such as Amazon, Microsoft, or Google and contact victims through email or LinkedIn.

Example Scam Message:

"Congratulations! You have been shortlisted for an Amazon remote opportunity. To activate your employee account please complete verification by paying a refundable onboarding fee."

Warning Signs:

  • Email domain does not match the official company website
  • Recruiter uses Gmail or Outlook
  • Immediate job offer without interview

Equipment Purchase Scam

Overview: Victims are hired remotely and asked to purchase work equipment from a specific vendor.

Example Scam Message:

"You will need to purchase the company approved laptop and software license from our vendor. Once payment is completed, we will reimburse you after onboarding."

Warning Signs:

  • Employer asks you to buy equipment yourself
  • Vendor website looks suspicious
  • Payment requested before official onboarding

Telegram / WhatsApp Recruitment Scam

Overview: Scammers recruit victims through messaging apps and offer quick online jobs.

Example Scam Message:

"Hello! We are hiring part-time remote workers. You can earn ₹5000 daily by completing simple online tasks."

Warning Signs:

  • Recruiter only communicates via Telegram or WhatsApp
  • Unrealistic daily income promises
  • Requests to deposit money to unlock tasks

Fake Overseas Job Scam

Overview: Victims are offered high-paying international jobs but must pay visa or processing fees.

Example Scam Message:

"You have been selected for a job opportunity in Canada. Please pay the visa processing fee of $300 to begin your immigration process."

Warning Signs:

  • High salary overseas jobs with minimal qualifications
  • Requests payment for visa processing
  • No official recruitment agency verification
